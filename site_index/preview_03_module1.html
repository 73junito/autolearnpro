# Multimeter Basics

Learning Objectives

  • Identify multimeter functions: DC voltage, continuity, resistance. - Take basic DC voltage and continuity readings safely.

Materials

  • Digital multimeter (DMM) - Vehicle battery or 12 V power source - Test leads, backprobe pins

Step-by-step Procedure

  1. Inspect the multimeter and leads for damage; set to the correct measurement (DCV for battery). 2. Connect the black lead to COM, red lead to V/Ω input. 3. For DC voltage: place red probe to positive terminal, black probe to negative/ground. Expect ~12.4–12.8 V for a resting automotive battery. 4. For continuity: set meter to continuity/beep mode, place probes across the component or wire; a beep indicates a low-resistance path.

Safety: Do not measure resistance on a live circuit — remove power before measuring ohms or continuity.

Examples

  • Battery voltage: 12.6 V (healthy resting battery) - Shorted circuit: continuity beep across connector pins indicates low resistance

Quick Check

  • Demonstrate a voltage reading and record the value.

Instructor Notes

  • Emphasize correct meter range and input selection to avoid meter damage.

# Electrical Fundamentals — Basic Theory

Learning Objectives

  • Explain voltage, current, and resistance in simple terms. - Apply Ohm's law to compute expected voltages or currents for basic circuits.

Key Concepts

Voltage (V)

  • The electrical potential difference; measured in volts.

Current (I)

  • Flow of charge measured in amperes (A).

Resistance (R)

  • Opposition to current, measured in ohms (Ω).

Ohm's Law

$$V = I \times R$$

Worked Example

  • If a sensor circuit draws 0.2 A through a 10 Ω resistor, V = 0.2 × 10 = 2.0 V.

Practical Notes

  • Many automotive sensors use low-voltage reference circuits; always verify manufacturer specs.

Instructor Notes

  • Include a short practice calculating simple series/parallel values.

# Lab: Multimeter Practice — Safe Measurements

Objective

Practice taking DC voltage, continuity, and resistance measurements on benign circuits and record results.

Setup

  • Work in pairs in the shop area with instructor supervision. - Equipment: DMM, test leads, sample wiring harness or bench power supply, 12 V lamp or resistor.

Tasks

  1. Measure the resting battery voltage and record the value. 2. Build a simple series circuit (battery, resistor, lamp) and measure voltage across and current through the resistor. 3. Use continuity mode to verify harness connectors and identify open circuits.

Deliverable

  • Upload a photo of the multimeter showing the measured voltage for task 1. - Submit a short text entry with recorded values for task 2 and a brief interpretation.

Safety

  • Disconnect the battery when changing wiring. Use insulated tools when necessary.
