Fuel Systems & Injection

Advanced • 10 lessons

Overview

This intensive course provides diesel technicians with hands-on mastery of modern fuel system components and diagnostics. Students dissect and rebuild critical components like high-pressure pumps, injectors, and fuel rails using authentic diesel-specific parts. Through extensive lab work, learners operate diagnostic tools to analyze pressure waveforms, detect leaks, and troubleshoot complex injection systems in common rail and mechanical setups. Practical sessions focus on precise measurements, component replacement, and system commissioning. By the end, graduates will confidently diagnose fuel delivery issues, perform preventative maintenance, and execute repairs on diesel engines, ensuring optimal performance, efficiency, and emissions compliance in real-world automotive settings.

Modules

  1. Fuel Pump Types
  2. Injection Principles
  3. Pressure & Flow Testing
  4. Calibration Methods
  5. Troubleshooting Common Failures

Learning Outcomes

  • Understand core diesel engine principles and components.
  • Perform basic diagnostics and routine maintenance tasks.
  • Apply workshop safety and best-practice procedures.
